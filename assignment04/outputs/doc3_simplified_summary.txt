The Dow and S&P 500 were down about one per cent from the start of the year, while the Nasdaq was just points higher. In New York, the Dow Jones industrial average lost 551.80 points or 2.2 per cent to 24,465.64. The January crude contract was down US$3.77 at US$53.43 per barrel and the December natural gas contract was down 17.7 cents at US$4.52 per mmBTU. Losses were led by energy, which dropped 2.9 per cent, followed by industrials, consumer discretionary, and financials. That's down 1,332.13 points from the start of the year and 10.3 per cent below the July peak.
